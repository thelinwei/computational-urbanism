# Glossary — formal language vs. private shorthand

A term used loosely in early thinking is not always the term that belongs in
a formal research proposal. This page keeps the two honest and separate,
rather than letting the more evocative language quietly drift into
methods sections where it would overclaim.

## Use in formal proposals

| Term | What it means here |
|---|---|
| **Threshold field** | A pedestrian threshold space (void deck, covered walkway, playground edge, hawker rim, lift lobby) treated as a node in a spatial graph, not just a location. |
| **Threshold affordance** | The material capacity of a space to support lingering, waiting, sitting, eating, playing, gathering — inferred from proximity to relevant infrastructure. A spatial capacity claim, not a behavioural one. |
| **Threshold transmissibility** | Whether affordance potential propagates through connected pedestrian-network segments, rather than existing only at isolated points. A networked capacity claim, not a claim about what actually spreads between people. |
| **Relational holding** | The human-observed outcome — co-presence, recognition, informal care, low-stakes encounter — that only fieldwork (observation, situated accounts) can establish. Not yet measured in this repo. |
| **Graph-diffused affordance exposure** | The formal name for the diffusion model in the landing page's Plate 01: local affordance scores diffusing through a weighted graph of connected segments. |
| **SVI-visibility / SVI-invisibility** | Whether a covered segment is plausibly within reach of road-based street-view imagery, per Pilot 01's distance-to-road proxy. |
| **Spatial autocorrelation / Moran's I / LISA** | Standard spatial-statistics vocabulary for whether a variable clusters in space more than chance would predict, and where. Used in Pilot 03 to test network-topology weights against simple-proximity weights. |

## Private generative shorthand (not for the proposal)

| Term | Why it's useful privately | Why it stays private |
|---|---|---|
| **Spatial contagion** | Memorable, and gestures at something real: architecture as a medium that modulates what kinds of relational life can spread. | "Contagion" implies disease-model dynamics applied to trust, care, or comfort — claims this research doesn't make and can't yet support with open data alone. |
| **Social weather** | Evokes the felt, ambient quality of how a space holds people. | Not an operationalisable variable; risks reading as poetic rather than methodological in a committee review. |

**The discipline, in one line:** discipline what the private language is a
contagion *of*. Contagion of a measurable, observable thing (SVI-visibility,
affordance clustering) is standard spatial statistics. Contagion of "care" or
"trust" is where a claim turns soft. Keep the formal writing anchored to the
left column; let the right column stay a private compass, not a public claim.
